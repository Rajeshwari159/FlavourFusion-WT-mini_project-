Pav Bhaji

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as carrots, peas, cauliflower, capsicum), finely chopped
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon pav bhaji masala
  • Pav (bread rolls) - 8 pieces
  • Butter for toasting the pav

Procedure

1. Heat oil and butter together in a large pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
3. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy, stirring occasionally.
5. Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
6. Add the mashed potatoes and mixed vegetables to the pan. Mix everything together until well combined.
7. Using a potato masher or the back of a spoon, mash the vegetables while they're cooking. Add a little water if the mixture looks too dry.
8. Let the bhaji simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all the vegetables are cooked well and the flavors are well incorporated.
9. In the meantime, slit the pav horizontally, but not all the way through.
10. Heat a griddle or a pan. Spread butter on the pan and toast the pav on both sides until they are lightly browned and crisp.
11. Once the bhaji is ready, garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a dollop of butter.
12. Serve the hot pav bhaji with lemon wedges and extra butter on the side.
Enjoy your homemade pav bhaji! Adjust the spices according to your taste preferences.
